You are given a clinical case report and a biomedical research abstract.

Your task is to analyze them together and identify what new clinical or biological questions become answerable only when considering both texts jointly.

CASE REPORT:
{patient_text}

RESEARCH ABSTRACT (Title: {title}):
{article_text}

Instructions:

1. Determine whether the abstract is meaningfully related to the case report.
If not related, output exactly:
No meaningful joint question could be found.

2. If meaningfully related, formulate 5 candidate questions that:
- cannot be answered by the case report alone
- cannot be answered by the abstract alone
- require integrating information from both texts

Each question must:
- be self-contained
- be phrased generically (e.g., “a patient” instead of “this patient”)
- apply to a class of patients with similar characteristics
- focus on mechanisms, disease interpretation, diagnostic reasoning, or clinical implications
- connect at least two specific clinical features from the case report
- avoid vague phrasing

Do not refer to “this patient” or “the patient”.

3. Evaluate the 5 questions and select the single strongest question based on depth of integration between the texts.

The selected question should:
- require integrating clinical observations with biomedical knowledge
- involve mechanism-level reasoning or treatment implications
- not be answerable using general textbook knowledge alone

4. Output:

Question:
<the selected question>

Explanation:
Explain:
- what clinical information motivates the question
- what biomedical knowledge enables answering it

Do not refer to “the abstract” in the explanation.
