You are given a clinical case report and a biomedical research abstract.

Your task is to generate one high-quality clinical question that can only be answered correctly by combining information from both texts.

CASE REPORT:
{patient_text}

RESEARCH ABSTRACT (Title: {title}):
{article_text}


Step 1

Determine whether the abstract is meaningfully related to the case report.

If they are not meaningfully related, output exactly:

No meaningful joint question could be found.


Step 2

If they are meaningfully related, generate one clinical decision-style question that requires integrating information from both texts.

The question must:

- ask about exactly one specific medical entity
- have a single-entity answer
- be self-contained (Do not refer to “the abstract”.)
- be phrased generically (for example: “a patient with …”, not “this patient”)
- include at least two patient-specific constraints from the case report
- describe a scenario where the patient-specific constraints are necessary to determine the correct answer
- not be answerable correctly using the case report alone
- not be answerable correctly using the abstract alone


The answer should be a concrete medical entity such as:

- a drug
- a diagnostic test
- a biomarker
- a mutation
- a procedure
- a specific treatment strategy with a clear name


Do NOT output answers that are:

- numbers
- ages
- yes/no answers
- vague treatment categories
- mechanism descriptions without a named entity
- multiple entities


Prefer questions about:

- treatment selection
- diagnostic test selection
- biomarker-based decisions
- mutation-guided therapy
- contraindication-aware management
- comorbidity-aware management
- pregnancy-aware treatment decisions


Avoid mechanism-only questions unless the answer is a specific named biomarker or mutation.


The patient-specific constraints included in the question must be necessary to determine the correct answer.

If removing those constraints would not affect the answer, generate a different question.
